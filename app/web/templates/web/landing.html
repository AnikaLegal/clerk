FAQ

Can you help with my rental situation?
If you're renting in Victoria and dealing with something like eviction, urgent repairs, a bond issue or a rent increase, we may be able to help.
We focus on supporting renters who are experiencing financial stress or have difficulty accessing legal services.
The best way to find out is to check your eligibility. It only takes a few minutes.
Is the service really free?
Yes, our legal support is completely free.
We're a community legal centre, which means we're here to help people who might not otherwise be able to access legal advice.
There are no hidden fees or charges.
How do I know if I'm eligible?
You may be eligible if you're a Victorian renter living in a private rental or social housing and:
  • Are on a low or middle income
  • Need help recovering your bond, seeking urgent repairs, or have received a Notice to Vacate
  • Live with disability or mental health challenges
  • Are experiencing family violence
  • Find it hard to access legal help elsewhere
If you're unsure, that's okay. You can still check your eligibility and we'll guide you through it.
What happens after I submit my case?
Once you submit your details, our legal team will review your situation.
We'll then get in touch with you to talk through your options and next steps.
We know these situations can feel stressful, so we aim to respond as quickly as we can.
